# PostSnail Branding

PostSnail is creator-owned microblogging with a handmade, proof-aware pixel-art identity. The brand should feel small, fast, accountable, and warm without becoming childish or platform-slick.

## Core Rules

- Use the supplied pixel-art PostSnail logo as the source of truth.
- Use the snail-only mark for favicon, app icons, compact nav, and small badges; use the wordmark/logo when the product needs to be recognized quickly.
- Keep pixel edges crisp with `image-rendering: pixelated` where raster logos are displayed.
- Prefer square corners, hard outlines, visible focus states, compact controls, and blocky accent bands.
- Avoid gradients as the main brand expression, blurred glow effects, rounded-pill overload, stock imagery, mascot redraws, and stretched or recolored logos.

Use coral for primary PostSnail actions, deep navy for proof/structure, warm paper for public pages, and restrained green for Forest registry/search surfaces.

## Tone

Write plainly and with ownership language: creator-owned, local-first, static, signed, verifiable, public proof, discovery hints. Do not overclaim privacy, security, identity, truth, decentralization, or FIPS validation. Say "post-quantum signed fingerprint" rather than "quantum-proof JavaScript."

## Surfaces

- Public website: expressive, logo-forward, explanatory, search/creation CTAs visible in the first viewport.
- Admin: compact, task-first, browser-only, no login, no required backend.
- Forest: discovery/search/registration, green accents, clear trust boundary that indexed results are summaries and proof files remain authoritative.
- Media kit: mirror these rules and link downloadable logo/icon assets.

## Accessibility

Keep text contrast high, focus rings visible, controls at least 38px tall, long hashes/URLs wrapping, and mobile layouts free of horizontal overflow.
